Amber Palace

Overview

"Amber Palace, also known as Amer Fort, is a stunning example of Rajput architecture located in Rajasthan, India. Built in the late 16th century by Raja Man Singh I, a trusted general of Mughal Emperor Akbar, the palace showcases a blend of Hindu and Mughal architectural styles. Construction of the palace began in 1592, and it was completed in the early 17th century. The palace was the main residence of the Rajput kings until the capital was moved to Jaipur. Amber Fort served as a strategic military stronghold and was well fortified, with large walls and watchtowers. The palace complex consists of several magnificent buildings, including the Diwan-e-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-e-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), and the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), each adorned with intricate carvings and beautiful frescoes. Amber Palace is renowned for its stunning architectural features, including the ornate mirror work in the Sheesh Mahal and the use of local materials such as red sandstone and white marble.
